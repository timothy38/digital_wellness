Stay Safe and Well in the Digital World

Understand Digital Wellness and Cyber Safety to Take Control of Your Online Life

What is Digital Wellness?

Digital wellness is about maintaining your mental, emotional, and physical health in relation to how you use technology. It's about being in control of your device use instead of letting it control you. Many people struggle with compulsive phone use, social media addiction, and digital fatigue without realizing it. Recognizing the signs and taking action is the first step toward a healthier digital lifestyle.

Signs of poor digital wellness include:
  • Sleep problems due to late-night scrolling, especially on social media or video platforms
  • Decreased attention span from constant switching between apps and tabs
  • Social isolation despite constant online presence, due to less meaningful in-person interactions

What is Cyber Safety?

Cyber safety involves protecting your devices, identity, and information while navigating the digital world. The internet is filled with threats that can compromise your personal data, privacy, and financial security. Cybercriminals often use deception, such as fake emails and websites, to trick users into giving away information.

Common online threats include:
  • Phishing emails and fake websites that mimic trusted sources to steal personal info
  • Malware and ransomware that infect devices and demand payment to restore access
  • Risks from using unsecured public Wi-Fi networks that expose your data to hackers

Tips for a Safer & Healthier Digital Life

  • Enable night mode to reduce eye strain
  • Set screen time limits
  • Take regular breaks from screens to prevent burnout
  • Use app timers to stay in control of your usage

  • Always use two-factor authentication (2FA) for important accounts
  • Regularly update your software and apps
  • Never click on suspicious links
  • Do not share personal info with unknown sources

  • Avoid doomscrolling by limiting time on news apps and social media
  • Mute or unfollow toxic accounts
  • Spend time on offline hobbies or in nature
  • Use “Do Not Disturb” or focus tools to reduce distractions
